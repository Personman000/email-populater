Step 3: Write your email














Use a clear subject line that focuses on the issue you’re covering in your email. Use key words like: apartheid, ongoing violence, human rights abuses, violation of international law, illegal dispossession of homes, violent attacks, etc.


Engage your MP with something short and to the point. State your purpose in the first 1-3 sentences. You can use a compelling fact or statement for impact.


Share who you are and why you care. It’s important that you use your own words - MPs are more likely to listen when the message is personalized.


You can:




Explain the problem. Highlight key details, present the causes of the problem and how widespread or serious it is. Cite your sources. Need help? Review our sources or read existing templates.


Choose an MP from Step 2 to see the options.


Ask your MP to commit to specific actions. Give a rationale for your request and demonstrate the urgency of the issue. It’s best to keep it to 2 - 4 actions.



Conclude your email with a demand that the MP respond to you. You can ask for more information about your MP’s stance. If you’re comfortable, ask to call or meet with them.
